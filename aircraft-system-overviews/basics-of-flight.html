Basics of flight

On this page we will cover the basic essentials of flight: thrust, lift and drag

  • Thrust

    Thrust comes from the engines and is used to propel the aircraft forward. In turbine engines different sections of the engine work to generate the thrust it takes to move the aircraft forward.

    • Intake

      Lets air into the engine.

    • Compression

      As the air flows through different stages of the compression section it gets more and more compressed.

    • Combustion

      Mixes the air with fuel, sending it through the turbine and out of the exhaust, creating the thrust needed to move the aircraft forward.

  • Lift

    Lift is generated from the airflow traveling across the wings

  • Drag

    When a flight surface moves out of its stowed position the drag created across the surface allows for control of the aircraft in the air. Feel free to check out Flight Controls - By Wire for more.
